Madison Square Garden

Statistics

  • Capacity: 19,738
  • Built: February 11th, 1968
  • Location: Manhatten, NY
  • Value: $1.1 billion

Other Notable Facts

MSG has been the home of the Knicks since their foundation in 1946. The building is known as "The World's Most Famous Arena" and "The Mecca of Basketball". The first version of it was built in 1879 to host track and field. It's fourth version is what the knicks play in now which was completed in 1968. It has been rennovated multiple times since then and is the most valuable stadium in the NBA. In addition to the Knicks, MSG is also home to the New York Rangers and the New York Liberty and hosts many concerts (A lot of Billy Joel), professional wrestling matches, college basketball games and other events throughout the year.

Metlife Stadium

Statistics

  • Capacity: 82,500
  • Built: April 10th, 2010
  • Location: East Rutherford, NJ
  • Value: $1.6 billion

Other Notable Facts

Metlife Stadium is part of the meadowlands sports complex in East Rutherford New Jersey. When the building was completed it was the most expensive stadium in America. The buildings design was inspired by the look that the skyscrapers in NYC have, although the stadium isn't really physically appealing at all. Metlife stadium also hosts the New York Jets and is one of three stadiums in America that houses two teams from the same professional sports league. It is the third most expensive stadium in the NFL and can seat the 8th most amount of people. Both the Giants and Jets have been bad for the last decade leading to bad times for most of the hometown fans that want to watch their team.

UBS Arena

Statistics

  • Capacity: 17,113
  • Opens: November 20th, 2021
  • Location: Elmont, NY
  • Value: $1.1 billion

Other Notable Facts

For their whole history the Islanders have played at Nassau Coliseum. Despite an attempt to renovate their old arena the Islanders were still pressured to move out of it because it was simply just too old and not up to par with other modern arenas in the US. Nassau Coliseum hosted the Islanders when they one four straight Stanley Cups between 1980-1983 and the stadium has a lot of sentimental value to the fanbase. Because of this the interior of UBS Arena has been modeled to capture the same atmosphere as Nassau Coliseum. It will be built with a low cieling to emulate the extremely loud atmosphere created by the low cieling at Nassua Coliseum.
